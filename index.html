About

The world, as I see it

I'm Ruban Kumar Shankar — a photographer drawn to the quiet drama of everyday life. Between the ordinary and the overlooked, there's a photograph waiting to be made.

My work moves across portrait, street, fashion, and analogue film — each frame a small act of attention. I shoot with instinct, edit with restraint, and believe that the best images don't explain themselves.

"Photography, for me, is not about capturing what is there. It is about staying present long enough to see what nobody else noticed."
— Ruban Kumar Shankar

The Practice

Analogue soul,
digital eye

I began shooting on film — the grain, the patience, the cost of every frame. That discipline never left me. Whether I'm holding a 35mm SLR or a mirrorless body, I still compose as though the roll is almost finished.

I work across portrait, documentary street, and editorial fashion — drawn most often to the in-between moments: the glance before the laugh, the stillness after the rain.
